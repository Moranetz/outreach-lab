# Brief — Modal Labs

## In one sentence

Modal sells serverless GPU + containerized compute to early-stage AI startups that need to ship features without owning infrastructure — same-day onboarding, per-second billing, scales to enterprise contracts.

## The buyer

The founder or first engineer at a 5–50 person AI-product startup. They've already chosen the "serverless > self-host" pattern. They read Hacker News, ship multiple times a day, decide on a vendor in 2–4 weeks based on devex and cold-start latency more than price. The check-signer is usually the founder; the technical champion is whoever first deployed a function.

## Why this seat fits Marion

- She IS the buyer Modal sells to — an indie iOS founder who has shipped Claude into 17 apps and would have chosen Modal if she'd needed serverless GPU. Empathy with the ICP is the unfair-advantage move.
- Her Closing Evidence Atlas signals research rigor that resonates with engineering-led GTM (Modal's GTM is famously engineer-friendly).
- `outreach-lab` is open-source sales tooling — a credible signal that she'd build internal tools instead of asking for them.
- She ships. Modal's culture rewards shipping. The 17-app portfolio is more legible to a Modal hiring manager than 5 years at Salesforce would be.

- Compensation may be heavily equity-weighted at this stage; verify base + OTE before investing application effort.
- If Marion needs structure + mentorship from day one, Modal's "figure it out" culture could be harder than a Series B with a real sales manager.
