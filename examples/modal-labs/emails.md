## How to pick

| If… | Use | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cold first-touch, no warm intro, recipient is hiring | Variant 1 (gain-framing) | Tightest posterior; lowest-risk variant in the Atlas |
| You have a warm intro lined up | Variant 5 (commitment-consistency) | Strongest mechanism for moving warm → meeting |
| You can name a real, verifiable gap in their outbound | Variant 2 (loss-framing) | Don't use unless the gap is real |
| Recipient explicitly asked about ramp/comp | Variant 4 (extreme-anchor) | Reply-only; anchor on a 90-day proof window |
| You have specific named peers who'd vouch | Variant 3 (social-proof) | Atlas flags this as high-variance; use only with real names |

After sending, log it:

```bash
outreach-lab track modal-labs \
  --variant v1 \
  --technique gain-framing \
  --subject "Built sales tooling for my Modal application" \
  --recipient "Head of GTM" \
  --response none
```

Update with `--response opened` / `replied` / `meeting-booked` when the response (or non-response) comes in. After 30 sends, `outreach-lab analyze` produces the cohort response-rate table.
